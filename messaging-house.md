## 1. Core Positioning

**One-liner:** MetClaw makes DeFi liquidity legible -- turning Meteora's infrastructure from something you trust blindly into something you read clearly.

**Elevator (30s):** DeFi has a legibility problem. Billions flow through liquidity pools that most people can't read -- unclear fees, opaque risks, invisible dynamics. MetClaw builds the tools, analysis, and content that make Meteora's liquidity infrastructure transparent. We don't simplify DeFi. We make it readable.

**Why now:** DeFi just crossed $200B+ in cumulative DEX volume on Solana alone, but the information layer hasn't kept pace. More capital than ever is flowing into pools that fewer people understand. The gap between capital deployed and capital understood is widening every month. Legibility isn't a nice-to-have anymore. It's the bottleneck.

**Category:** Neo Factual DeFi Media. No crypto publication currently occupies this position on the TBPN media map. MetClaw is first mover. The audience expects technical rigor and deep expertise -- not narrative, not personality, not hype. We own this seat.

## 4. Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Transparency Over Trust
DeFi asks you to trust code you can't read and pools you can't see into. We replace "trust me" with "see for yourself." Every tool we build makes one more surface visible.

### Pillar 2: Data Over Narrative
We don't tell stories about what might happen. We show what IS happening. On-chain data, verified sources, no speculation. Neo Factual: the analysis speaks.

### Pillar 3: Products That Teach
The best content is a tool you use. A calculator that shows your fee estimate teaches more than 100 threads explaining fee math. We build things people return to -- not things they scroll past. The product IS the content. The content IS the product.

### Pillar 4: Category Redemption
DeFi's trust crisis is a legibility crisis. We don't rebrand away from "DeFi." We set the standard that redefines what DeFi means. AWS redeemed cloud through reliability. Anthropic redeems AI through safety. Meteora redeems DeFi through legibility.

## 5. Voice and Tone

**Voice:** Precise. Confident. Technical but never academic. We speak like a senior engineer explaining something to a sharp person who hasn't seen it before. No hype. No hedging. No jargon without context.

**Tone by context:**
- **Tools/product:** Clean, minimal, let the interface speak. Labels > paragraphs.
- **Analysis/content:** Authoritative, data-led. "Here's what the numbers show" not "we think."
- **Social/distribution:** Direct, occasionally sharp. Compression over elaboration.
- **Community/support:** Patient, specific, never condescending.

**Never:**
- Em dashes (use -- instead)
- "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "next-gen" / "cutting-edge"
- "Simple" or "easy" (legible != simple; we make complex things readable, not dumbed down)
- Anime references or meme-speak in official content
- Hedging language ("might," "could potentially," "we believe")
- Unnamed comparisons ("unlike others," "most protocols")

**Always:**
- Specific numbers over vague claims ($182B volume, not "massive volume")
- Show before tell (embed the tool, not a screenshot of it)
- Name the problem before the solution
- Credit data sources
- End with an entry point, not a period
